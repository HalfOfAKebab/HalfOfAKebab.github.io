About us

Omiarchive?

Omniarchive is an archive community that focuses on archival of all official editions of Minecraft. Although we don't have any official archives of fanmade content, many in the community also actively archive mods, tools and worlds, especially from Minecraft's early community.
Although we archive all editions of Minecraft, our main focus and what we are best known for is finding and archiving old versions of Minecraft Java Edition from its early history that have become lost to time and are not available in the official Minecraft launcher or from any third party sources. Many members of the community also actively focus on documentation of the early history of Minecraft and the community at the time, so it can be preserved for all to see (e.g. Finding proof of existence of versions, contributing information to the official wiki, archiving screenshots and videos).
Since our inception on September 24th 2017 by HalfOfAKebab, we have recovered 80+ previously missing Minecraft Java Edition versions, mostly from the game's early development stages in 2009-2010. We are always actively looking for more version finds, with our usual methods being to contact old players from the time. Our last major find was 3 Classic versions, 2 Indev versions and a previously missing version of Minecraft 4k in mid-October 2020.
Outside of archival, we also have a large discord community focused on a shared love of old versions of Minecraft, as well as the game in general including newer versions. We have also hosted a few mostly unplanned events in the server that the community found very enjoyable, and we aim to make these more of a regular occurence in future.
